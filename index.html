As of March 2021, 168 million children globally were out of school for almost a full year due to the Covid-19 pandemic.

Throughout the pandemic, schools responded in a number of different ways:
some shutting down entirely, while others switched to hybrid learning, and yet others remained entirely open.

However, many children around the world faced issues accessing education even before the pandemic.

UNESCO defines a country's out-of-school rate as:

the proportion of children in the official age range for the given level of education
who are not enrolled in pre-primary, primary, secondary or higher levels of education



Out of school rates vary widely across the globe. Explore the map on the next page to find the variation in out-of-school rates across regions.

So What Can Be Done?


  • In addition to funding new schools and teachers to keep up with population growth, any aid to address out of school rates must focus on the causes that keep vulnerable children out of school.
  • According to UNICEF, these causes include: pervasive levels of poverty, protracted conflicts, and complex humanitarian emergencies.
  • Investments should focus on regions such as the Middle East and North Africa, which have faced high out-of-school rates due to frequent wars.
  • Additionally, high levels of poverty in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia would make these regions prime targets for aid.
